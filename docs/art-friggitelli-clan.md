## The Rejection of the Blacksmith

### The Blacksmith's Way (Rejected)

From Ecclesiastes (paraphrased):
*"Working until hungry, weak, thirsty, and faint just to make a tool"*

**Characteristics**:
- Pounding (force)
- Exhaustion
- Depletion
- Suffering as requirement for creation
- Breaking yourself to build something

This is the path to burnout. This is unsustainable. This is Fission.

### The Baker's Way (Embraced)

**Characteristics**:
- Kneading (gentle pressure)
- Rising (exponential growth through yeast)
- Warmth (cultivated heat, not destructive fire)
- Nourishment (the output sustains)
- Creating something delicious

The bread rises on its own once you've kneaded the knowledge into it. You don't have to pound it into submission.

## The Salmon of Knowledge

From Irish mythology: Finn (Fionn) burned his thumb while cooking the Salmon of Knowledge, put his thumb in his mouth, and instantly absorbed all wisdom.

**"Kissy fishy mommy's hungry feed me need me knead me knowledge in the bread"**

- **Kissy fishy**: The Salmon (knowledge)

You don't pound knowledge into yourself. You knead it, let it rise, bake it, and it becomes nourishment.

## Finn-Again: The Loop Structure

**Finnegan** = Fionn (bright/fair) + Again (resurrection/restart)

This is the Fin-Again principle:
- **Fin**: The end, completion, collapse
- **Again**: The restart, rebirth, new cycle
